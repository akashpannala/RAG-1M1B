PLASTIC WASTE MANAGEMENT RULES 2016 - INDIA

Key Provisions:
1. Waste Segregation at Source
   - All waste generators must segregate plastic waste
   - Separate collection of different plastic categories
   - Penalties for non-compliance

2. Thickness Standards
   - Plastic carry bags must be minimum 50 microns
   - Increased from 40 microns to prevent littering
   - Thicker bags are more reusable

3. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)
   - Producers responsible for collection and disposal
   - Must establish collection systems
   - Annual targets for plastic waste collection

4. Ban on Multi-Layered Plastics
   - Complete ban from September 2018
   - Non-recyclable multi-layered packaging prohibited
   - Alternatives must be used

SWACHH BHARAT WASTE SEGREGATION GUIDELINES

Three-Bin System:
- Green Bin: Wet/Biodegradable waste (food scraps, garden waste)
- Blue Bin: Dry/Recyclable waste (paper, plastic, metal, glass)  
- Red Bin: Hazardous waste (batteries, electronics, chemicals)

Household Implementation:
- Segregate waste before disposal
- Municipal workers collect separately
- Different processing for each category
- Fines for improper segregation

BUREAU OF ENERGY EFFICIENCY - CONSERVATION GUIDELINES

Residential Energy Saving:
1. Lighting
   - Replace all bulbs with LED (75% energy saving)
   - Use natural daylight during day
   - Install motion sensors in less-used areas
   - Turn off lights when leaving rooms

2. Air Conditioning
   - Set temperature to 24°C or higher
   - Clean filters every 2 weeks
   - Use ceiling fans along with AC
   - Ensure proper insulation of rooms
   - Service AC annually

3. Refrigeration
   - Keep refrigerator away from heat sources
   - Maintain 3-inch gap from walls
   - Don't keep hot food inside
   - Defrost regularly
   - Check door seals

4. Water Heating
   - Use solar water heaters where possible
   - Set geyser temperature to 45-50°C
   - Turn off when not needed
   - Insulate hot water pipes

5. Appliances
   - Buy 5-star rated appliances
   - Unplug devices when not in use
   - Use washing machines with full loads
   - Air dry clothes instead of dryers

Star Rating System:
- 5 Star: Most efficient (best choice)
- 1 Star: Least efficient (avoid)
- Check BEE label before purchase
- Higher star = lower electricity bills

CENTRAL POLLUTION CONTROL BOARD - AIR QUALITY GUIDELINES

Air Quality Index (AQI) Categories:
- 0-50: Good (minimal impact)
- 51-100: Satisfactory (minor issues for sensitive people)
- 101-200: Moderate (breathing discomfort for sensitive groups)
- 201-300: Poor (breathing discomfort for most people)
- 301-400: Very Poor (respiratory illness on prolonged exposure)
- 401-500: Severe (affects healthy people, serious impacts on those with diseases)

Personal Actions to Reduce Air Pollution:
- Use public transport or carpool
- Switch to electric/CNG vehicles
- Avoid burning waste or leaves
- Plant trees (one tree absorbs 22kg CO2/year)
- Regular vehicle maintenance
- Use energy-efficient appliances

NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL (NGT) KEY DIRECTIVES

Construction & Demolition Waste:
- Must be processed and reused
- 20% of road construction must use recycled materials
- Violations attract penalties up to ₹1 lakh

Electronic Waste (E-Waste):
- Cannot be disposed with regular waste
- Must be given to authorized recyclers
- Manufacturers must take back old devices
- Informal recycling is illegal

Water Conservation:
- Rainwater harvesting mandatory for buildings >300 sqm
- Groundwater extraction requires permission
- Industries must treat and reuse wastewater
- Penalties for water pollution

PRADHAN MANTRI UJJWALA YOJANA (PMUY)

Objectives:
- Provide clean cooking fuel to poor households
- Reduce indoor air pollution from traditional fuels
- Improve women's health and reduce drudgery
- Prevent deforestation

Impact:
- 9+ crore LPG connections provided
- Reduced firewood use by 60% in beneficiary households
- Significant health improvements reported
- 80% reduction in time spent collecting firewood

INDIVIDUAL ACTIONS FOR SUSTAINABILITY

Daily Habits:
- Carry reusable bags for shopping
- Use steel/glass bottles instead of plastic
- Refuse single-use plastics (straws, cutlery)
- Compost kitchen waste at home
- Buy local and seasonal produce

Transportation:
- Walk or cycle for short distances
- Use public transport when possible
- Maintain vehicle properly for efficiency
- Plan trips to reduce fuel consumption
- Consider electric vehicles

Water Conservation:

- Use bucket instead of shower (saves 50L/bath)
- Install aerators on taps
- Reuse RO reject water for plants
- Collect rainwater where possible

Consumer Choices:
- Buy products with minimal packaging
- Choose products from sustainable brands
- Repair items instead of replacing
- Donate or recycle old items
- Avoid fast fashion

- Not segregating waste: ₹500-1000 fine

- Industry violations: ₹1-5 lakh fine

- No PUC certificate: ₹1000-10000
- Industrial emissions: ₹1-10 crore

Water Pollution:

Reporting:
