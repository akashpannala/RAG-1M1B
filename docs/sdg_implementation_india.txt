INDIA'S SDG IMPLEMENTATION FRAMEWORK

SDG 11: SUSTAINABLE CITIES AND COMMUNITIES

Urban Challenges in India:
- 35% population lives in urban areas (480 million people)
- Rapid urbanization (3-4 cities per year)
- Housing shortage of 10 million units
- Traffic congestion and air pollution
- Inadequate public spaces

Government Programs:
1. Smart Cities Mission
   - 100 cities selected for smart development
   - ₹48,000 crore investment
   - Focus: digital infrastructure, efficient services
   - Progress: 6,000+ projects completed

2. AMRUT (Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation)
   - Water supply to all households
   - Sewerage and septage management
   - Storm water drains
   - Green spaces and parks

3. Metro Rail Expansion
   - 18 cities with metro systems
   - 700+ km operational
   - Target: Reduce private vehicle use by 30%

Achievements:
- 100% street lighting in smart cities
- WiFi hotspots in public areas
- Integrated command and control centers
- Improved citizen services delivery

SDG 12: RESPONSIBLE CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION

Waste Generation in India:
- 62 million tonnes of waste annually
- Only 70% collected
- 15% processed
- Rest goes to landfills
- E-waste: 3.2 million tonnes/year

Circular Economy Initiatives:
1. Plastic Waste Management
   - EPR for producers
   - Plastic waste collection increased by 25%
   - Ban on single-use plastics (July 2022)

2. E-Waste Management
   - Collection targets for manufacturers
   - Authorized recyclers network
   - Awareness campaigns

3. Construction & Demolition Waste
   - Mandatory recycling
   - Use in road construction
   - Processing facilities in cities

Resource Efficiency:
- National Resource Efficiency Policy
- Industry standards for water and energy
- Promote zero waste manufacturing
- Circular economy business models

SDG 13: CLIMATE ACTION

India's Climate Commitments:
- Net zero by 2070
- 50% energy from renewables by 2030
- Reduce emissions intensity by 45% (from 2005 levels)
- Create additional carbon sink of 2.5-3 billion tonnes CO2

National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC):
Eight missions:
1. Solar Energy
2. Enhanced Energy Efficiency
3. Sustainable Habitat
4. Water Conservation
5. Sustaining Himalayan Ecosystem
6. Green India (afforestation)
7. Sustainable Agriculture
8. Climate Knowledge

Climate Finance:
- ₹35,000 crore for renewable energy
- Green Climate Fund projects
- International Solar Alliance
- Climate adaptation fund for states

Adaptation Measures:
- Drought-resistant crop varieties
- Efficient irrigation systems
- Disaster early warning systems
- Coastal protection infrastructure
- Urban heat action plans
